Transcribing a YouTube video that's playing? Read this first

This recorder listens to your microphone, not the audio playing in another tab — that's a browser security limit, not a bug here. To transcribe a video's audio, route your speaker output into a virtual microphone so the mic "hears" what's playing:

  1. Windows: right-click the speaker icon → Sound settings → More sound settings → Recording tab → enable Stereo Mix (right-click in empty space → "Show Disabled Devices" if it's hidden). No Stereo Mix option? Install a free virtual audio cable app (e.g. VB-Audio Virtual Cable) instead, and set it as your playback device so audio routes through it.
  2. In Chrome/Edge, click the 🔒 lock icon in the address bar on this page → Site settings → Microphone → choose Stereo Mix (or your virtual cable) as the input device, then reload this page.
  3. Play the video, then hit Start recording here. Keep your system volume up so the mix has signal — muting speakers mutes the mic feed too.

Tip: switch your input device back to your regular microphone afterwards for normal voice dictation.
